The Crust of it All

What's a perfect pie without a perfect piecrust? Do you like that flakey goodness or soft and chewey? Whatever you like, there are 4 basic types of pie crusts just for you!

  1. Flakey
  2. Sweet
  3. Crumb
  4. Mealy

Pans

A heavy glass or pyrex pan is ideal for a great pie. Its durable, you can easily check the pie for doneness, and it won't scratch. Glass also won't react with the different fruit acids in the fillings, that tend to effect the pie's flavor.

Metal pans though are ideal selection. they are light weight and easy to store. Metal pan's dont break easily as glass pan's can . Choose the right type of metal pan though. A darker pan will absorb more heat than a shiny pan will.

Lastly we have ceramic pans. These tend to be more decorative and eye catching. However, ceramic pans behave well in the oven with their great heat transfer and retention. You will have to test out the baking a few times though, because cooking times and browning can be vastly different than conventional pans.

Crusts

Typically the homemade apple pie uses a flakey crust topped in a cross hatch pattern. Sometimes it is covered with cutouts in the shape of leaves or other type of ornamental shapes. However there is more to our crust than the name alone. The type of flour, and fat (butter, lard, vegetable shortning) that you use will have a great impact on whether your pie is a golden delight or a messy brown flop. Other ingredients like salt, sugar, eggs, and the liquid in the filling also go a long way to make the pie crust a success.
